when I reviewed the yeast data in cabiommh I noticed that the entries for the engineered genes of the yeast models were not correct. The engineeredgene table in cabiommh contained entries such as leu, ade2, and ade3 for some of the strains. Although these modifications are listed in the official nomenclature of the yeast strains used in the DTP experiments I would rather stick to the modifications that were described as significant in the documentation on the DTP website. http://dtp.nci.nih.gov/yacds/exp_design.html
 
I gathered the following information from the DTP website:
Six yeast strains were used for testing in stages 0 and 1. Each strain contains modifications of 4 or 5 genes. All six strains have modifications in the genes pdr1, pdr3, and erg6. In addition to these modifications three strains contain a modification of a single gene: rad50, bub3, and mec2. The other three strains contain 2 additional modifications: CLN2oe + rad14, mlh1 + rad18, and mgt1 + sgs1
 
 
Testing in stage 2 was performed on 11 genetically modified strains and two strains which served as wildtype controls.
Each of the genetically modified strains contains 4 modified genes: the afore mentioned pdr1, pdr3, and erg6 genes and one additional gene.
The additional modified genes are:
bub3 (also used in stages 0 and 1)
CLN2oe
mec2 (also used in stages 0 and 1)
rad14
rad18
rad50 (also used in stages 0 and 1)
rad52
mgt1
mlh1
rad50EPP+
sgs1
 
 
The entries ( engineeredgene_id >=3000) of the engineeredgene table of the cabiommh database should be replaced by the data in the attached Excel spreadsheet.
 
Once the data have been replaced in the engineeredgene table in cabiommh they can be imported into camoddev. The import instructions are altered in that they should say now "import all data starting from engineeredgene_id 3000 including 3058 "
